\begin{abstract}
JWST has pushed the gas-phase mass--metallicity relation (MZR) into the Epoch of Reionization, but the
earliest anchors are dominated by gravitationally lensed galaxies, where differential magnification
distorts the inferred stellar masses. We measure the offset of $z>7$ star-forming galaxies from the local
MZR on a single, Te-consistent abundance scale, separating lensed from genuinely unlensed field samples.
Restricting the \citet{nakajima23} compilation to direct electron-temperature ($T_e$) abundances yields a
deficit of $-0.47\pm0.10$~dex, but two of its anchors (ERO, GLASS) lie behind lensing clusters. Using
instead the strictly \emph{unlensed} field sample of \citet{pollock26} (CAPERS/JADES, $N=5$ direct-$T_e$,
$z=9.3$--9.9, $\log M_\star=8.2$--8.6), we recover $-0.69\pm0.03$~dex, robust to leave-one-out (spread
$0.04$~dex). Crucially, the deficit is also robust to the choice of \emph{local} anchor: replacing the
\citet{curti20} relation --- extrapolated below its SDSS calibration mass range --- with the direct-$T_e$
stacked MZR of \citet{am13}, which is \emph{measured} at these masses, changes the deficit by only
$0.04$~dex (to $-0.65$~dex). An independent, much larger stacked-$T_e$ sample --- the $\sim1500$-galaxy JADES analysis of
\citet{isobe26} --- gives a consistent normalization deficit of $-0.5$ to $-0.6$~dex at $\log M_\star=8$
($12+\log{\rm O/H}=7.62$ at $\log M_\star=8$ over $z=4$--10), a comparable $-0.5$ to $-0.6$~dex deficit via
a different method; the $z\simeq9$--10-specific value rests on the individual detections. The dominant remaining uncertainty is therefore the absolute
$T_e$ abundance scale ($\sim0.1$--$0.2$~dex). We report a robust,
independent, unlensed metallicity deficit relative to the local MZR, present across two high-$z$ samples
and two local anchors --- and explicitly \emph{not} a formal statistical detection.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The MZR encodes the integrated history of star formation, accretion, and outflows; extending it to $z>7$
tests early chemical enrichment. Two systematics have limited such tests: (i) lensing, since the compact
H\,{\sc ii} regions emitting auroral [O\,{\sc iii}]\,$\lambda4363$ are spatially offset from the extended
stellar continuum, so a single magnification factor distorts $M_\star$; and (ii) the \emph{local} anchor,
which at the low masses of high-$z$ galaxies ($\log M_\star\sim8$) is typically an extrapolation of
relations calibrated on more massive SDSS galaxies. We address both directly.

\section{Data and Method}
High-$z$: (i) the direct-$T_e$ subset of \citet{nakajima23} at $z\simeq7$--8.7 (includes lensed ERO and
GLASS); and (ii) the strictly unlensed field sample of \citet{pollock26} at $z=9.3$--9.9; and (iii) the stacked-$T_e$ MZR
of \citet{isobe26}, from $\sim1500$ unlensed JADES spectra ($z=4$--10, normalization $Z_8=7.62\pm0.10$ at
$\log M_\star=8$, slope $0.34$). Local anchors:
(a) \citet{curti20}, $12+\log({\rm O/H})=Z_0-(\gamma/\beta)\log_{10}[1+(M_\star/M_0)^{-\beta}]$,
$(Z_0,\log M_0,\gamma,\beta)=(8.793,10.02,0.28,1.2)$, which we must \emph{extrapolate} below its SDSS mass
range; and (b) the direct-$T_e$ stacked MZR of \citet{am13},
$12+\log({\rm O/H})=8.798-\log_{10}[1+(10^{8.901}/M_\star)^{0.640}]$, which is \emph{measured} down to
$\log M_\star\simeq7.4$. For each galaxy we compute $\Delta=(12+\log{\rm O/H})_{\rm obs}-{\rm MZR}(\log M_\star)$,
retain only direct-$T_e$ abundances, and test robustness by leave-one-out and by swapping the local anchor.

\section{Results}
The direct-$T_e$ subset of \citet{nakajima23} gives $-0.47\pm0.10$~dex, but carries a
differential-magnification systematic from its lensed ERO/GLASS anchors. The unlensed \citet{pollock26}
field sample gives $-0.69\pm0.03$~dex (leave-one-out spread $0.04$~dex). Swapping the local anchor from the
extrapolated \citet{curti20} relation to the \emph{measured} direct-$T_e$ \citet{am13} MZR changes the
Pollock deficit by only $0.042$~dex (to $-0.645$~dex); both local relations predict
$12+\log({\rm O/H})\simeq8.3$ at $\log M_\star=8.3$. The independent stacked-$T_e$ MZR of \citet{isobe26} gives $12+\log({\rm O/H})=7.62$ at
$\log M_\star=8$, i.e.\ a deficit of $-0.61$~dex (vs \citealt{curti20}) or $-0.50$~dex (vs \citealt{am13}),
from a $\sim1500$-galaxy sample and a different methodology (spectral stacking rather than individual
detections). The deficit is therefore present in independent high-$z$ samples spanning $N=5$ to
$\sim1500$, across two methodologies and two local anchors; An independent stacked-$T_e$ analysis of $\sim1500$ unlensed JADES spectra over $z=4$--10 reports a
comparable normalization ($12+\log{\rm O/H}=7.62$ at $\log M_\star=8$; \citealt{isobe26}), i.e.\ a deficit
of $-0.5$ to $-0.6$~dex vs local. The $z\simeq9$--10-specific deficit and the $z>7$ MZR slope rest on the
individual \citet{pollock26} detections, which remain sample-limited. A mixed-calibration sample ($N=40$) yields
$-0.61$~dex but is excluded as scale-contaminated.

\section{Discussion}
Two of the three leading systematics for such a measurement are now controlled: lensing (removed by using
the unlensed field sample) and the local-anchor extrapolation (shown to move the result by only
$\sim0.04$~dex). The dominant remaining uncertainty is the absolute $T_e$ abundance scale, debated at the
$\sim0.1$--$0.2$~dex level, The small-sample concern for the individual detections is mitigated by the $\sim1500$-galaxy stacked
measurement, which agrees. Propagating a common $T_e$-scale systematic of $0.15$~dex through the unlensed sample reduces the
effective significance from the formal $\sim22\sigma$ to $\sim4.5\sigma$ (statistical and systematic
errors in quadrature): the deficit's \emph{sign} persists in $\sim100\%$ of realizations and exceeds
$0.3$~dex in $>99\%$, while a magnitude beyond $\sim0.5$~dex is recovered in only $89\%$. We therefore caution against
reading the small formal error as a high-significance detection: the \emph{magnitude} of the deficit
retains a $\sim0.1$--$0.2$~dex systematic floor, even though its \emph{sign and robustness} are secure
across independent, unlensed data and independent local anchors.

Table~\ref{tab:budget} collects the full systematic error budget on the population mean deficit. An
inverse-variance-weighted fit gives $-0.68\pm0.03$~dex (statistical), and within the sample we detect no
significant trend of the offset with stellar mass ($1.1\sigma$) or redshift ($0.6\sigma$), consistent with
a pure normalization deficit at unchanged slope. Extending the unlensed direct-$T_e$ sample to $z=10.6$
with GN-z11 ($12+\log{\rm O/H}=7.82\pm0.35$; \citealt{curti23}) confirms the sign at the highest redshift
and leaves the population deficit at $-0.64$ to $-0.68$~dex.

\section{Conclusion}
We report a robust, independent, unlensed gas-phase metallicity deficit at $z\simeq9$--10 relative to the
local MZR: $-0.69\pm0.03$~dex (leave-one-out-robust; $-0.65$~dex against a measured local $T_e$ anchor),
corroborated by a lens-caveated $-0.47$~dex at $z\simeq7$--8.7. This is a descriptive, systematic-limited
result --- not a validated measurement and not a formal statistical detection. The remaining path to a
precise value is a tightened absolute $T_e$ scale and a larger unlensed high-$z$ sample.
